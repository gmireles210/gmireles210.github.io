LiDAR Avalanche Susceptibility of Mt. Hood, OR.
Spatial Modeling of Point Cloud Data

3D Avalanche Susceptibility

Background

For this lab I decided to create an avalanche susceptibility map for Mt. Hood, Oregon. The reason why I choose this idea is that I am an avid snowboarder and several times throughout the year when I am up in the mountains either here in Washington or Oregon, I see avalanche signs and hear active blasts in the surrounding area. This is an important process as it clears snow from the mountains that could cause an avalanche, possibly destroying structures and/or killing people in its path. At the end of this analysis I would like to know the areas that are used for recreation during the winter that are most prone to avalanches.
To help guide me through the process of creating an avalanche susceptibility map, I utilized several scholarly journals to help develop best practices for conducting an analysis such as this. The journals are, “An ArcGIS Geo-Morphological Approach for Snow Avalanche Zoning and Risk Estimation in the Province of Bergamo,” by Barbara Marana and “Spatial modelling of snow avalanche run-outs using GIS,” by Biskupic, Marek and Barka, Ivan.




Analysis


An avalanche analysis requires various data and analytic computing platforms to include LiDAR data in the form of a LAS dataset of Mt. Hood, a Digital Elevation Model (DEM) and Digital Surface Model (DSM) of Mt. Hood, Aspect and Slope of the LAS dataset, Contour Lines, ArcMap, ArcScene, and the ability to perform raster analysis using algebraic expressions. Both journals mentioned above provide best methods and indexes to use when mapping avalanche areas. To my surprise, a few items do remain consistent throughout both, such as the “degree of slopes” and “aspect of slopes” when mapping for avalanche prone areas.
This consistently remains true for almost all avalanche situations as avalanches occur when in the same directions (aspect), regardless of area and slopes are the same way, once a certain degree of slope is met, avalanches occur. This specific type of knowledge really helped me understand the parameters to set inside of ArcMap and ArcScene in order to get the most accurate representation at the end of the analysis.
